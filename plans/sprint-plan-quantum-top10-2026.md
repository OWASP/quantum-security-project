## Overview

The OWASP Quantum Security Project published a v0.1 draft of the Top 10 for Quantum Security Risks at launch. That draft is a bootstrap intended to open the conversation, not a finished list. Every entry is open to challenge, revision or replacement.

This plan sets out how we move from that draft to a community-validated v1. All sprints run Monday to Monday.

The list spans two surfaces:

- **Migration surface (QS01-QS07):** the cryptographic transition
- **Platform surface (QS08-QS10):** quantum platforms and hybrid quantum-classical systems

**OWASP Slack:** `#project-quantum-security`
**Email:** contribute@quantum-owasp.org

## Pre-Sprint 0: Generative sprint and formation
**20 July - 3 August 2026**

**Objective:** Open the floor. This is the phase in which the shape of the list is determined.

### Activities
- Distribute the markdown entry template for 2026 entries
- Publish the evidence and anchor convention: standards, peer-reviewed venues, CVEs, and demonstrated proofs-of-concept
- Open submission of new entries via pull request to the proposals directory
- Open feedback on the existing v0.1 entries via pull request, or via issues where the feedback is less formed
- Recruit contributors across both surfaces
- Share this plan with the community

### How to contribute
- **New entry proposals:** pull request, using the standard markdown template
- **Structured feedback on existing entries:** pull request against the entry
- **Less formed feedback, questions and discussion:** raise an issue

Contributors do not need to be entry leads or working group members to submit.

### Deliverables
- Templates and contribution model in circulation
- A repository of proposed new entries
- Structured feedback on the v0.1 draft entries

## Sprint 2: Development
**17 August - 7 September 2026**

**Objective:** Develop the candidate entries in full.

### Activities

- Full development of each entry: definition, scope boundaries, current examples, actionable mitigations
- Anchor each entry to its supporting evidence

### Deliverables
- Entry leads named
- Ten fully developed entries (v0.3)

## Sprint 4: Public review

## Working principles

**The v0.1 entries hold no incumbency.** They were published as a bootstrap. They compete alongside new proposals in Sprint 1 voting on the same terms, and any of them may be revised, merged or dropped.

**Evidence over speculation.** Proposals are tagged as demonstrated, emerging or theoretical, so that the evidence base of the final list is visible rather than implied.

**Both surfaces matter.** The migration surface draws on a large practitioner community. The platform surface does not, and contributions there are actively sought.

---

## Success criteria

The v1 list should be:

- Useful to defenders today
- Actionable and practical
- Linked and grounded to existing guidance
